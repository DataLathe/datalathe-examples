Example output from running MultiTableAiExample with three CSV files
(customers, orders, products) and a relationship prompt that tells the
LLM how the tables join together.

Run command:
  mvn exec:java \
    -Dexec.mainClass=com.datalathe.examples.MultiTableAiExample \
    -DapiKey=$DATALATHE_AI_KEY

Engine: localhost:3000
Provider: anthropic
Run took ~21s end-to-end (4 AI calls + chip create/delete).


=== Q1 ===
Q: What is the total revenue by region?

SQL: SELECT c.region, SUM(o.quantity * o.unit_price) as total_revenue
     FROM orders o JOIN customers c ON o.customer_id = c.customer_id
     WHERE o.status = 'completed'
     GROUP BY c.region ORDER BY total_revenue DESC

             region, ranked from highest to lowest.

=== Q2 ===
Q: Show me revenue and profit by product name.

SQL: SELECT p.product_name,
            SUM(o.quantity * o.unit_price)             as revenue,
            SUM(o.quantity * (o.unit_price - p.cost))  as profit
     FROM orders o JOIN products p ON o.product_id = p.product_id
     WHERE o.status = 'completed'
     GROUP BY p.product_name ORDER BY revenue DESC

Explanation: Total revenue and profit for each product from completed
             orders, ordered by revenue from highest to lowest.

=== Q3 ===
Q: Who are the top 3 customers by revenue? Show their name, region,
   total revenue, and how many distinct products they ordered.

SQL: SELECT c.name, c.region,
            SUM(o.quantity * o.unit_price) as total_revenue,
            COUNT(DISTINCT o.product_id)    as distinct_products
     FROM orders o JOIN customers c ON o.customer_id = c.customer_id
     WHERE o.status = 'completed'
     GROUP BY c.customer_id, c.name, c.region
     ORDER BY total_revenue DESC LIMIT 3

Explanation: Top 3 customers ranked by total revenue from completed
             orders, including their region and the number of distinct
             products they purchased.

=== Q4 (follow-up — model resolves "top customer" from prior turn) ===
Q: For the top customer, break down their orders by product with
   quantity and total spent.

SQL: SELECT p.product_name,
            SUM(o.quantity)                 as quantity,
            SUM(o.quantity * o.unit_price)  as total_spent
     FROM orders o JOIN products p ON o.product_id = p.product_id
     WHERE o.status = 'completed'
       AND o.customer_id = (
           SELECT c.customer_id FROM customers c
           JOIN orders o2 ON c.customer_id = o2.customer_id
           WHERE o2.status = 'completed'
           GROUP BY c.customer_id
           ORDER BY SUM(o2.quantity * o2.unit_price) DESC LIMIT 1)
     GROUP BY p.product_name ORDER BY total_spent DESC

Explanation: Product breakdown for the highest revenue customer,
             showing quantity ordered and total amount spent per
             product.

Enterprise Platform | 2        | 5000
DataSync License    | 5        | 2250
